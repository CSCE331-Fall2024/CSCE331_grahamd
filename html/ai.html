Approaching Constructional Drawing

Introduction

Constructional drawing is a fundamental technique used by artists to build complex forms from simple shapes. It involves breaking down objects into basic geometric forms and reconstructing them to create accurate and proportionate drawings.

Major Concepts

  • Basic Shapes: Using spheres, cubes, cylinders, and cones as building blocks.
  • Perspective: Mastering one-point, two-point, and three-point perspective to create depth.
  • Proportion and Scale: Maintaining correct proportions while scaling objects.
  • Light and Shadow: Applying shading techniques to give form and dimension.

Resources

  • Drawabox - A free resource for learning the basics of constructional drawing.
  • Proko - Tutorials and courses on figure drawing and anatomy.
  • Ctrl+Paint - Digital painting lessons that cover foundational drawing skills.

Popular Artists

  • Andrew Loomis: Known for instructional art books focusing on figure drawing and construction.
  • Kim Jung Gi: Renowned for his ability to draw complex scenes from imagination using construction techniques.
  • Scott Robertson: Specializes in drawing and designing with an emphasis on industrial and transportation design.

Conclusion

By mastering constructional drawing, artists can improve their ability to draw complex subjects accurately and confidently. It serves as a strong foundation for any artistic endeavor.
