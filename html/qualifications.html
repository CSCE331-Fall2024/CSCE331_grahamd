Computer Science as a Medium

My technical area of interest in Computer Science is computer graphics rendering and software optimization. The thought that in some way we can continuously improve everything we make, bit by bit, through either direct algorithmic approaches or generalized organizational methods is extremely interesting to me. Sure, no program is perfect, but in the same way there is no problem too big to be solved. Once some abstract and tangled structure is carefully unraveled, it can eventually be understood. To maintain a skill sharp enough to dissect and use these highly sophisticated programs for the betterment of others is what inspires me to continue learning.

While difficult, the puzzle put before us in Computer Science isn’t just the problem statement, it's how we can use the medium of software to resolve this real world issue. Following this, computer graphics interests me because it is a method of developing software that tackles these issues. Computer graphics typically reminds people of video games, in which I don’t blame them, but there are many creative applications of this concept that we use daily, without even recognizing. From using CAD software to seeing the applications of VR and AR in medical, industrial, and business fields, making systems that provide a layer of abstraction through visualization is very powerful.

Through both software optimization and graphics rendering, I aim to make tools that aid others in their daily lives, founded by the unnecessary struggles that we face daily. Following my Mechanical Engineering past, I can’t help but recognize sub-optimal solutions to daily problems that, with the knowledge that computer science provides me, I can solve. This ideology was the birth of many automated scripts, smaller programs, and eventually the creation of the KavTools website. I wish to bring this mindset to other platforms, providing optimized, well-maintained tools to whoever may need them.
